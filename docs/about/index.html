About the Lake Health Project

Monitoring and protecting our local lake ecosystem through community science

Our Mission

The Lake Health Project is a community-driven scientific initiative dedicated to understanding and improving the health of our local lake ecosystem. Through systematic monitoring, data analysis, and community education, we aim to:

  • Identify factors contributing to algae growth and water quality issues
  • Develop sustainable solutions for lake management
  • Engage community members in citizen science and environmental stewardship
  • Advocate for evidence-based policies to protect our water resources

Project Origins

Our project began after the community experienced unprecedented algae growth during the summer months. Concerned about the ecological impact and potential health risks, local residents came together to find solutions.

Under the leadership of local scientist Julianne Taylor, we initiated a comprehensive monitoring program to:

  • Measure key water quality parameters (temperature, pH, ORP, dissolved oxygen, phosphorus, nitrogen)
  • Survey vegetation coverage and diversity
  • Conduct wildlife surveys using camera traps
  • Document human usage patterns and their environmental impact

Over three years, we're collecting data to understand the lake's ecosystem dynamics and develop targeted interventions.

Our Approach

We believe in a science-based, community-driven approach to lake management:

Scientific Rigor

We use standardized protocols and calibrated equipment to ensure data quality and comparability over time.

Community Engagement

From data collection to decision-making, community members are involved at every stage of the project.

Sustainable Solutions

We prioritize mechanical and natural solutions over chemical treatments for long-term ecological health.

Our Goals

Through this project, we aim to achieve:

  1. Comprehensive Data Collection: Build a robust dataset on lake health parameters over multiple seasons
  2. Community Education: Empower residents with knowledge about lake ecology and monitoring techniques
  3. Evidence-Based Recommendations: Develop science-backed strategies for lake management
  4. Policy Influence: Advocate for funding and regulations that support lake health
  5. Long-Term Monitoring: Establish ongoing monitoring programs for sustained ecosystem health
