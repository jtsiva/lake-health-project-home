What is the project?

The community grew concerned after a summer of “unprecedented algae growth” on our lake. Our local scientist, Julianne Taylor, is now performing ecological monitoring for at least 3 years. She'll be measuring water quality parameters (temp, pH, ORP, dissolved oxygen, phosphorus, & nitrogen) and also surveying vegetation coverage, conducting herp surveys in the spring, and using camera traps to identify wildlife using the lake. The community’s ultimate goal is to limit the algal growth during the summers without having to continually dump chemicals in the lake. To that end, we'd like to procure funding from the state for the mechanical removal of the noxious weeds in the lake.

Want to learn more?

We've put together this course to help learners of all ages and background familiarize themselves with the skills and knowledge involved in this scientific endeavor. The course is organized into three major categories: data collection, data analysis, and science communication. As the project is ongoing, these will be completed once the steps has been completed in real life. If you are visiting this course after the project is completed then you are in luck!
