Goal: Comprehensive assessment of the lake ecosystem health including sampling & analysis of:

  • habitat structure
  • organism structure (phytoplankton, macrozoobenthos diversity, macrophyte coverage)
  • sample water to identify chemicals, bacteria, oxygen levels, and nutrients
  • monitoring wildlife usage of lake

In order to properly manage a lake supporting both animal life and recreational use, we need to have a thorough understanding of how the lake is functioning and how healthy it is. This will help us better understand any water quality issues that arise, the root cause of the issue, and the most effective treatment. Monitoring the lake health will ensure we continue to uphold or improve the water quality to ensure longevity of the ecosystem services provided by our lake (recreation, wildlife habitat, wildlife food, hydrological process integrity, ecosystem resilience). We can use the data in grant applications for funding restoration efforts, like removal of the invasive plants growing in the lake.
